The auditory layer

Why there's a beat on some of these

Rhythmic Auditory Stimulation is one of the best-evidenced tools in motor rehabilitation. Wiggle Room borrows it for the very start of the lifespan — carefully, and for three specific reasons.
1 · Primes
A steady pulse puts the motor system in a state of readiness to move. This is the mechanism behind rhythmic gait training in stroke, Parkinson's and cerebral palsy.
2 · Pulls
Infants from ~5 months move rhythmically more to music than to speech — and it's the beat, not the melody, that drives it. Better-matched movement came with more smiling.
3 · Paces you
The adult can lock to a beat, so the metronome times your bouncing, rocking and stepping — making your handling predictable and repeatable.
What this is not: we are not drilling babies to keep time. Infant movement is periodic but not beat-locked, and true synchronisation matures around age 10. The beat is a primer, a motivator and a scaffold for the caregiver — never a test the baby can fail.
